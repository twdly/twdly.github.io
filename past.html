Past

This page is about the history of my experiences with computing so far.

Early Days

My passion for computers, like many others I'd assume, began with playing games from a young age. I still remember watching Dad play Crash Bandicoot 2 on our PS2 which was the newest console for the time. The first console that I ever owned myself was a DS Lite which I used to put an extensive amount of time into various games across numerous franchises with the most notable ones being Mario and Pokemon.

Growing Interests

Sometime during 2015 or 2016, I stumbled across a video of someone building a PC. Although I can no longer find the video, I distinctly remember it involving the person building the PC spray painting all the components green. It was this video that made me want to build my own computer. Several years later, at the end of 2019, I had successfully researched and procured the parts to my first PC build with the specs as follows:

Component Part

My PC (and the mess on my desk) the day I built it.

Since I initially built my PC, I have been continually upgrading it in numerous aspects with the most notable upgrades being adding a 1tb Samsung 970 EVO Plus NVMe SSD and changing my graphics card out for an Asus Dual RX 6600XT. If you are interested at a more comprehensive look at my current specs, you can find my specs here. If you have never built a computer before and you've even slightly considered it, I implore you to put the time in and do it because it is a highly rewarding process with a result that you can use every single day.

Programming at School

Throughout my time at school, I had always taken opportunities to learn how to program wherever possible which resulted in me learning quite a few programming languages and other technical skills. In Year 6, we were given an opportunity to do any project we had an interest in. With a partner, I began to learn basic JavaScript under the false belief that it was the langauge Minecraft was written in (and looking back at moments like that shows just how far I've come). After realising that JavaScript wasn't the language used to make Minecraft, we rectified our mistake and began learning basic Java. I remember enjoying the process of learning how programming languages work as it allowed me to appreciate just how much time and effort goes into the software that we take for granted. Unfortunately, I didn't keep using Java after the initial learning process and, as a result, promptly forgot everything I knew about it.

My programming adventure continued again in Years 9 and 10 when I took IST; an elective subject focused on various programming projects. Throughout my time in this subject, I learned how to use many different technologies such as HTML, CSS, Arduino, Unity, SteamVR Hammer and, most importantly, C#. A couple of the projects I did as a part of this course are outlined in the Past Projects table above.

After enjoying my time in IST, I decided to take Software Design and Development for Years 11 and 12. The most notable part of this subject was the requirement of completing a major work each year. It was during these major works that I furthered what I learned about C# in IST and applied it in a manner that allowed me to properly internalise how to program (somewhat) well. Once again, the projects created throughout this course are featured in the Past Projects table above.
